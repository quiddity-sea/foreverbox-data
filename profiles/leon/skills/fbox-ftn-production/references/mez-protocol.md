# Mez Protocol — Full Writing Rules

## Rule 1: No Em Dashes or En Dashes
**NEVER use "—" or " – " in any FTN content.**

Em dashes ("—") and spaced en dashes (" – ") are not UK English punctuation conventions. Spaced en dashes are American-influenced typesetting and are explicitly prohibited — they are not a loophole or acceptable substitute.

Replace with commas, colons, semicolons, full stops, parentheses, or rewrite the sentence.

Search every draft for both "—" and " – " before declaring it done.

**Examples:**

NO: "The report was damning — it confirmed every allegation."
YES: "The report was damning. It confirmed every allegation."

NO: "MI5 — the security service — lied to the courts."
YES: "MI5, the security service, lied to the courts."

## Rule 2: Real Quotes Only
Never invent, approximate, or paraphrase a quote and present it as direct speech.

**Quote attribution levels:**
1. Primary source confirmed: you have read the original document. Attribute directly.
2. Secondary source only: you are quoting from a news outlet's coverage of a document you have not read. Attribute to the outlet, not the primary source.
3. No verbatim available: use reported speech.

**Cross-checking:**
- Financial figures must be verified against the most recent coverage.
- Judicial quotes must match the original word for word. Check small drifts ("to" vs "on", extra adjectives).
- When in doubt, attribute to the reporting outlet.

## Rule 3: UK English Only
Colour, organise, centre, programme, realise, behaviour, labour, travelled, cancelled.

## Rule 4: No Eyewitness Claims
Analytical and archival voice only. Never imply physical presence.

## Rule 5: Six-Day Sourcing Window
Trigger events from sources published within 6 days of target publish date.

## Rule 6: Theme Legible in Copy
Day theme or tagline explicit in body text for any post over 300 words.

## Rule 7: Signature
Every Substack post ends with: *Curated by Zeon7. Not a journalist.*
