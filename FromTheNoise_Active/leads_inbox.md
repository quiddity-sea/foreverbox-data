**Theme:** 404: Hope Not Found — 2026-07-16 (Thursday)

**Who:** Thalha Jubair (20, Bow, east London) and Owen Flowers (19, Walsall). Part of "Scattered Spider" hacking collective. Sentenced by Mr Justice Turner.

**The Signal:** Two teenagers living in a council flat and with a grandmother gained "the highest privileged access" to Transport for London's core IT systems. **Key Quote:** "At one point... Jubair and Flowers could have shut out and shut down TfL completely." — Prosecutors, as reported by The Guardian. Also: "primarily motivated by selfish bravado, heedless of the severe consequences to others" — Mr Justice Turner, sentencing remarks.

A critical public service — the transport backbone of a global city — was breached by two teenagers with no state backing, no military-grade tools, just bravado and a Telegram channel. TfL's only defence was unplugging. The cyberpunk aesthetic is baked in: "SCATTERED SPIDER IS CREATING WEBS ON THE UNDERGRND" is the kind of graffiti the noise age writes in code. The £39m figure makes it material. The disabled passenger angle makes it human. The sentencing today makes it current.
