**STORY TITLE:** MI5 Lied to Three Courts to Protect a Neo-Nazi Spy Who Attacked a Woman With a Machete

**PUBLISH DATE:** 2026-07-16

**SOURCING WINDOW:** 2026-07-10 to 2026-07-16

## TIMELINE

- **2020:** BBC Investigations correspondent Daniel De Simone begins investigating a violent neo-Nazi informant. A senior MI5 officer (Officer 2) discloses the man's agent status to De Simone in phone calls, trying to persuade him not to investigate. This breaches MI5's core "neither confirm nor deny" (NCND) secrecy policy.
- **2020-2021 (approx):** Agent X — the neo-Nazi informant — coercively controls his British partner (alias "Beth") and attacks her with a machete. MI5 later helps him go abroad to continue intelligence work while under police investigation.
- **Post-incident:** Beth takes legal action. MI5 gives evidence to three courts claiming it maintained NCND — that it never disclosed Agent X's status. Courts accept this and allow MI5 to keep information secret from Beth.
- **2025-02 (approx):** BBC reveals that MI5 lied to the courts. MI5 "vehemently denies" the allegation.
- **2025-09:** The Prime Minister orders an investigation by the deputy investigatory powers commissioner, Sir John Goldring, after MI5's explanations are rejected as "deficient and unreliable" by the High Court.
- **2026-07-16:** Sir John Goldring's report published. Confirms MI5 gave evidence based on "lies" to three courts. Heavily criticises senior MI5 figures. Findings: Officer 2 lied repeatedly, Officer 3 misled colleagues, Witness A (deputy director) overstated matters. Report could lead to contempt of court or criminal prosecution.

## KEY FACTS

- **Institution:** MI5 — UK's domestic security service
- **Agent X:** Foreign neo-Nazi, misogynist informant. Used spy status to coercively control his British partner "Beth." Attacked her with a machete.
- **The lie:** MI5 told three courts it maintained NCND (neither confirm nor deny) regarding Agent X's status. This was false. MI5 had disclosed his status to BBC journalist Daniel De Simone in 2020.
- **Consequence of the lie:** Courts allowed MI5 to keep information secret from Beth, the abuse victim.
- **MI5 Director General:** Sir Ken McCallum — "MI5 recognises without hesitation the seriousness of our failings... I repeat my previous apologies to both courts for the incorrect evidence that was provided, and for our slowness in recognising what had happened."
- **Home Secretary:** Shabana Mahmood — findings "stark." Taking "urgent action to hold MI5 to account."
- **Officer 2:** Senior MI5 officer who "put forward a wholly fictitious account" and "formed the foundation of MI5's false account." Lied repeatedly. Had tried to cover up for Agent X, falsely claiming he was "not an abusive misogynist nor a real extremist." Even wanted De Simone to meet the agent.
- **Officer 3:** Separate senior MI5 officer who "misled his own colleagues," "bears considerable responsibility for the continuation of MI5's falsehood," and "misrepresented" what Officer 2 said. Was not "truthful" about warnings received from colleagues.
- **Witness A:** MI5 deputy director who "overstated" matters and "contributed to MI5's continued reliance on the false account."
- **MI6 and foreign agency:** Both knew NCND had been departed from. MI5 still told courts otherwise.
- **Report finding:** "Serious and systemic failures in MI5's conduct." "Cogent evidence" in case files. "Opportunities to correct the position were missed."
- **Potential outcomes:** Contempt of court proceedings, possible criminal prosecution.
