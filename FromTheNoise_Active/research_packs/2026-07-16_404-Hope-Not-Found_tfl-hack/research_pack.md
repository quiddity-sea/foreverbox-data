# RESEARCH PACK

**STORY TITLE:** The Keys to the Kingdom — How Two Teenagers Broke London's Transport Network

**PUBLISH DATE:** 2026-07-16

**SOURCING WINDOW:** 2026-07-10 to 2026-07-16

## TIMELINE

- **2024-08-31 to 2024-09-03:** Thalha Jubair (then 18) and Owen Flowers (then 17) breach TfL's core IT systems over four days. They gain "highest privileged access" and create a "domain admin" account.
- **During the attack:** Dial-a-ride service for disabled passengers unable to process bookings. TfL unable to process Oyster/contactless payments or register Oyster cards.
- **End of attack:** TfL "pulls the plug" on its systems — only way to stop the breach.
- **Post-attack:** 27,000 TfL staff forced to reset passwords. Data of millions of commuters stolen.
- **2026-06 (approx):** Jubair and Flowers plead guilty.
- **2026-07-16:** Sentencing at court. Both receive 5.5 years each. TfL attack cost confirmed at £39m.

## KEY FACTS

- **Cost to TfL:** £39 million
- **Staff affected:** 27,000 forced password resets
- **Data breach:** Millions of commuters' data stolen
- **Service disruption:** Dial-a-ride (disabled passengers) unable to process bookings; Oyster/contactless payments disrupted
- **Hackers:** Thalha Jubair (20, Bow, east London — lived with parents in council flat) and Owen Flowers (19, Walsall — lived with grandmother and uncle)
- **Collective:** Part of "Scattered Spider," a loose English-speaking hacking group. NCA says convictions "effectively halted the group's criminal activity"
- **Method:** Breached to "highest privileged access," created domain admin account. Communicated via Telegram. Flowers livestreamed the attack.
- **Wealth:** Accrued millions of dollars in cryptocurrency through hacking activities
- **TfL Commissioner quote:** Andy Lord called it "the worst incident he had faced in his career"
- **TfL statement:** Attack could have caused "catastrophic damage" and "significant and extended transport service degradation and disruption"
- **Judge's finding:** "primarily motivated by selfish bravado, heedless of the severe consequences to others" — Mr Justice Turner
- **Potential severity:** Prosecutors said they "could have shut out and shut down TfL completely"
- **Celebrity search:** Hackers searched TfL's customer database for celebrities
