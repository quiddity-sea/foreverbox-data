# Story Lead Card Template

Use this exact format for every Story Lead Card appended to `leads_inbox.md`.

---

## SIGNAL: [Descriptive Title in Title Case]
* **Theme**: [Day Signal Name] ([Day]) — [Reflective/Personal/Investigative/etc.]
* **Trigger Date**: [YYYY-MM-DD] (source publication date — MUST be within 6-day window)
* **Who**: [Key actors, voices, communities, or individuals]
* **Where**: [Specific location(s) — neighbourhood, region, institution, digital space]
* **The Signal**: [2-3 sentences. Analytical insight, not summary. What is the pattern, the fracture, the quiet resistance? Connect to systemic frame.]
* **Key Quote**: "[Verbatim quote from source]" — [Author/Source, Publication, Date]
  * If no verbatim quote available: `None — reported speech only`
* **Angle for FTN**: [1-2 sentences. How does this fit the cyberpunk/folklore lens? What is the hidden mechanism? What are both sides ignoring?]
* **Source**: [Full URL] — [Publication name, Date]

## Example (from session 2026-07-08)

## SIGNAL: A Moment That Changed Me — The Telegram That Brought Clarity
* **Theme**: Out From the Noise (Wednesday) — Reflective, Personal
* **Trigger Date**: 2026-06-24 (within 6-day window to 2026-07-08)
* **Who**: Juliet Nicolson, writer; her father Nigel Nicolson
* **Where**: London / Kent / Hampshire (UK)
* **The Signal**: A father's letter — delivered by motorcycle messenger at considerable expense — arrived at a 22-year-old's crossroads: university or Tehran with a DJ boyfriend. The father said nothing in the moment, then wrote a letter weighing both paths with equal care. That letter became a decision framework the author used for five decades. The signal is not the choice but the witnessing: a parent modelling how to hold complexity without collapsing it.
* **Key Quote**: "Apart from guiding me towards the immense privilege of going to university, his letter changed the way I approached big decisions thereafter, weighing up the pros and cons of life's dilemmas with equal care. It also gave me a new confidence to just have a go, even if everyone (or almost everyone) tells you not to." — Juliet Nicolson, The Guardian, 24 June 2026
* **Angle for FTN**: The quiet architecture of resilience. In a culture that worships the disruptive pivot, this is the folklore of maintenance: a letter, a messenger, a father who trusted his daughter with the full weight of the dilemma. The cyberpunk frame: the message was analogue, expensive, deliberate — a packet sent across a noisy channel with zero packet loss. The folklore frame: the heirloom decision-tool passed down.
* **Source**: https://www.theguardian.com/lifeandstyle/2026/jun/24/moment-changed-me-telegram-arrived-choose-head-heart — The Guardian, 24 June 2026

## Validation Checklist (before appending)
- [ ] Trigger date within 6 days of target publish date
- [ ] Key quote is verbatim with traceable source (or explicitly marked as reported speech)
- [ ] The Signal is analytical, not descriptive summary
- [ ] Angle for FTN names the cyberpunk/folklore lens
- [ ] UK English spelling throughout
- [ ] Zero em dashes
- [ ] Source URL accessible and dated
